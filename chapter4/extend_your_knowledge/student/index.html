HWB summary:The HWB color model stands for Hue, Whiteness, and Blackness. It is similar to the HSL model, but instead of using saturation and lightness, it uses whiteness and blackness. In HWB, you define the hue (the base color), the amount of white (whiteness), and the amount of black (blackness). This allows for a simpler way of modifying colors based on how light or dark you want the color to be, providing a more direct way to achieve different shades of a color.

CMYK summary: >CMYK stands for Cyan, Magenta, Yellow, and Key (Black). It is a subtractive color model primarily used in color printing. In this model, colors are created by subtracting varying percentages of these four inks from the white color of the paper. The more ink is added, the darker the result. CMYK is ideal for printers as it reflects how colors mix when printed. Unlike RGB (used for screens), which is based on light emission, CMYK relies on ink absorption and light reflection.
